A Language That Endures

From its first day to the present day, education has been at the core of Qatar Foundation's (QF) mission, as it has nurtured change-makers and leaders, broadened minds, and shaped future paths within an ecosystem of knowledge like no other.

And so it was fitting that, as QF entered its 30th year, it played its part in marking International Day of Education: a global, United Nations-led celebration of the role of education in advancing peace and development.

Held under the theme 'Education is Everyone's Responsibility', QF's International Day of Education event – attended by Her Highness Sheikha Moza bint Nasser, Chairperson of Qatar Foundation – placed a focus on the enduring importance of the Arabic language in preserving cultural and social identity: from innovative approaches to teaching Arabic, to ensuring children connect with and embrace the language and the values it carries.

10
innovations focused on advancing pre-university education worldwide were honored through 'Spotlight on Qatar' – a competition by QF and global education organizer HundrED
"This is a step to promote Arabic not only as a means of communication, but also a bridge to science and knowledge, reflecting its civilizational and humanistic contributions."
— Sheikha Noof Ahmed bin Saif Al Thani, Executive Director of Strategic Initiatives at QF's Pre-University Education, speaking about the partnership between the Ministry of Education and Higher Education and QF

The event also announced the launch of a strategic partnership between the Ministry of Education and Higher Education and QF, aimed at supporting Arabic language education in international curricula through tools, programs, and resources, under QF's Rasekh initiative.

Social Progress

Engagement Through Discourse

The Arabic word 'jadal' means 'dialogue' or 'discourse' – and, in the context of the participation of Muslim women in public life, this is what QF's Al-Mujadilah Center and Mosque for Women fostered across three days in January.

Its inaugural Jadal research summit gathered Muslim women scholars, researchers, and practitioners from the Global North and South to explore its central topic through three lenses: Muslim women and the law, women's lives and history, and the wellbeing and health of Muslim women.

100+
delegates from North America, Europe, Asia, Africa, and the Middle East attended the inaugural Jadal summit

Speaking at the summit, Her Highness Sheikha Moza bint Nasser, Chairperson of Qatar Foundation, said: "Through Jadal, we honor the Qur'anic concept of jadal, as praised by Allah in Surat al-Mujadilah and embodied in the story of Khawla bint Tha'laba."

"The participation of Muslim women in religious scholarship and scholarship on Islam is more important now than ever before."
— Dr. Sohaira Z. Siddiqui, Executive Director, Al-Mujadilah

The story of Khawla bint Tha'laba (r.a.), who engaged in a dialogue with the Prophet Mohammed (PBUH), is the inspiration behind the name of Al-Mujadilah Center and Mosque for Women. The Jadal summit draws also from this legacy and aims to foster meaningful engagement.

Al-Mujadilah, established in 2024, aims to foster a Muslim society where all women are engaged, their participations and contributions are acknowledged and recognized, and their perspectives shape contemporary Muslim thought and discourse.

Sustainability

Learning from the Past

Alongside international days and international gatherings, January was also a month of international partnerships – as QF's Earthna Center for a Sustainable Future joined forces with The King's Foundation to showcase how the lessons of yesterday can contribute to a more sustainable tomorrow.

The two-year collaboration with the UK charity – founded by His Majesty King Charles III – will establish four urban development pilot projects around the world that demonstrate the vital role of traditional knowledge and local cultural heritage in shaping 21st Century sustainable practices.

The 4 pilot projects under the partnership will take place in:
  • India
  • Guyana
  • Sierra Leone
  • Tanzania

Announced at The King's Foundation's headquarters in Dumfries House, Scotland, the partnership has selected projects representing traditional forms of sustainability in different regions, with the insights they provide being shared with countries facing similar challenges – including Qatar.

"Our partnership with The King's Foundation stands on a shared belief: that the timeless wisdom of traditional knowledge, shaped by generations in harmony with nature, holds the key to building a sustainable future for our world," said Her Excellency Sheikha Hind bint Hamad Al Thani, Vice Chairperson of Qatar Foundation.

"We are looking forward to bringing our work in building sustainable communities and transforming lives to this new partnership."
— Kristina Murrin CBE, Chief Executive of The King's Foundation

"In safeguarding the cultural heritage of these four countries, we are also safeguarding invaluable knowledge and transforming it into a living resource for sustainable development."

Progressive Education

Learning Without Limits

The power of partnership was also seen in the sphere of inclusive, accessible education in January, with the inauguration of Warif Academy – the first specialized government academy of its kind in Qatar, specifically designed to ensure students with disabilities have the very best education.

"Warif Academy goes beyond traditional education by offering specialized educational and therapeutic services meticulously designed to address the unique needs of students with disabilities."
— Abeer Al Khalifa, President of Pre-University Education, QF

Established by the Ministry of Education and Higher Education, in partnership with QF, Warif Academy will ultimately cater for students aged 3-21 years – some of whom may not have had the opportunity to study before – through academic, behavioral, and therapeutic programs that empower as well as educate, and support services that address each student's individual needs.

25
classrooms by the 2028-29 academic year
150
students capacity

Speaking at Warif Academy's inauguration, Her Excellency Lolwah bint Rashid bin Mohammed Al Khater, Minister of Education and Higher Education, highlighted how its establishment marks a significant milestone in Qatar's ongoing commitment to fostering inclusivity and ensuring equality of opportunity, particularly for those with disabilities.

The 'Exploration and Challenge' camp was organized by:
  • QF's Pre-University Education
  • The Internal Security Force (Lekhwiya)
  • The Ministry of Endowments (Awqaf) and Islamic Affairs
  • The Qatar Scouts and Guides Association
  • Qatar Calendar House
  • Qatar Red Crescent

And the spirit of collaboration also saw students from schools within and beyond QF come together in January to develop their leadership and decision-making skills – and strengthen their sense of Islamic identity and community values – at the second 'Exploration and Challenge' camp, which, through separate sessions, featured the participation of girls for the first time.

Social Progress

The Voices of Contribution

At home and abroad, the need for the voice of youth to be heard in mapping the future of the Middle East and North Africa region was reinforced in January, by a QF-founded center dedicated to debate.

The World Economic Forum's Annual General Meeting 2025 in Davos, Switzerland, saw QatarDebate collaborate with Kurdistan House for a panel discussion focusing on the role of young people in shaping policy and diplomacy in the Middle East, and how regional partnerships are key to ensuring they have a seat at the table.

Meanwhile, in Doha, QatarDebate – together with the Ministry of Sports and Youth and Msheireb Museums – brought together youth and experts from across the Gulf for the GCC Youth Summit 2025, where topics ranging from cultural heritage preservation and climate change to technological development and economic diversification were placed in the spotlight.

"Through this summit, we hope that we will be able to develop effective strategies that contribute to empowering youth and enhancing their contribution to society."
— His Excellency Yasser Al Jamal, Undersecretary of the Ministry of Sports and Youth, speaking about the GCC Youth Summit 2025

"In a rapidly changing world, the youth of the region are not only facing significant challenges, but also creating innovative solutions that impact both their societies and the global community," said Dr. Hayat Abdullah Marafi, Executive Director, QatarDebate.

"The GCC Youth Summit is an ideal platform for these young leaders to showcase their potential and contribute effectively to vital issues."

Artificial Intelligence

Where Digital Meets Human

Within QF's Education City, the world's first university museum dedicated to media and communication can be found – and, in January, it was the venue for exploration of the continually evolving relationship between human creativity and machine learning.

The 'Ai or Nay? Artificial vs. Intelligent' exhibition at QF partner university Northwestern University in Qatar's Media Majlis Museum examined the intersections, and the boundaries, between digital and human learning, and how the journalism of today and tomorrow can harness Artificial Intelligence while navigating its drawbacks.

20+
works by regional and international artists featured in 'Ai or Nay? Artificial vs. Intelligent'
"The museum is shaping a new paradigm of cultural engagement in the region; one that centers community learning, interdisciplinary dialogue, and regional relevance."
— Marwan M. Kraidy, Dean and CEO of Northwestern University in Qatar, speaking about the Media Majlis Museum

And QF's Hamad Bin Khalifa University (HBKU) partnered with Quantum.Tech to bring its flagship quantum computing conference to the Middle East for the first time, with thought leaders in the field and multinational companies such as Microsoft, Deutsche Bahn, JPMorgan Chase, and Rolls Royce among those attending.

200+
experts from around the world attended the Quantum.Tech conference at HBKU

"HBKU and Quantum.Tech recognize that the Middle East is well-positioned to unlock the potential of quantum technologies and drive transformative impact for our economies and societies," said Dr. Saif Al-Kuwari, Director of HBKU's Qatar Center for Quantum Computing, as the three-day conference focused on topics including quantum innovations, commercialization, and adoption, as well as how cybersecurity can stave off quantum-powered attacks.

Precision Health

At the Forefront of Healthcare

Intended to address growing demand for innovative treatments for genetic diseases such as Spinal Muscular Atrophy and Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy, QF's Sidra Medicine – a leading women's and children's hospital and medical research hub – opened its new Gene Therapy Center as 2025 began.

"As a Qatari institution, our goal is to create a regional center of excellence, where children from across the Arab world can access advanced therapies without the need to travel long distances, bringing world-class care closer to home."
— Professor Tawfeq Ben-Omran, Division Chief of Genetic and Genomic Medicine, Sidra Medicine

Reinforcing Sidra Medicine's position at the regional vanguard of advanced care, which draws patients from across the Middle East and North Africa, the center – announced during the Arab Health 2025 conference – is earmarked to expand in the future to treat other rare and complex diseases.

45+
children have received groundbreaking gene therapies since Sidra Medicine launched its program in 2022

The center was unveiled as Sidra Medicine marked seven years of offering pioneering medical education, from residency and fellowship programs that nurture the clinicians of the future to some of the world's top conferences in this field.

400+
medical trainees have graduated from Sidra Medicine's pediatric residency and fellowship programs

"When Sidra Medicine's medical education pillar was established, our mission was to be ranked among the top academic medical centers in the world," said Professor Ibrahim Janahi, Acting Chief Medical Officer and Chair of Medical Education at Sidra Medicine. "I am pleased to see how far we have come on this journey and the big strides we have taken to develop the next generation of healthcare and research professionals."

Precision Health

Illuminating Genetics

On the theme of health, a major study on autism spectrum disorder among Qatar's population was published by HBKU's Qatar Biomedical Research Institute (QBRI), shedding new light on the genetic basis of the condition.

"These findings offer new insights into genetic factors affecting autism in Qatar, and may open new avenues for counseling and personalized treatment."
— Dr. Omar Albagha, Acting Executive Director, QBRI

Working with QF's Qatar Precision Health Institute and the Shafallah Center for Children with Disabilities, the research team used advanced computational tools to reveal 13 genes not previously associated with the risk of autism, laying the ground for further work on its genetic roots in the Arab world.

13
new genes identified associated with autism risk
50
children with autism, and their parents, enrolled in QBRI's genetic study

Meanwhile, the scientific expertise of a professor of physics at QF partner university Weill Cornell Medicine-Qatar (WCM-Q) helped a team of high school students from Qatar claim medals at a top international science competition – just one example of how universities at QF support young people beyond their own ranks of students.

34
WCM-Q alumni – specializing in fields including neurology, neonatology, psychiatry, surgery, and cardiology – joined the reunion event in New York

And many of the past beneficiaries of WCM-Q's world-class medical education reunited in New York to share their stories and expand their networks, at an event attended by alumni from each of the university's graduating classes from 2011 to 2024 who are now making their mark across the US medical world.

Innovation, Empowerment, Inclusivity

Elsewhere within QF's partner universities, three weeks of inspiring students to create startups focused on social entrepreneurship came to a close at Carnegie Mellon University in Qatar (CMU-Q), which debuted its Start for Good competition.

"We wanted to offer an educational experience that inspires and enriches students, and also generates ideas that can make a huge change in the world."
— Maher Hakim, Distinguished Career Professor in Business Administration, CMU-Q
The ideas of the 3 winning startups in CMU-Q's Start for Good competition were:
  • An eco-friendly bike-sharing program
  • A multisensory kit supporting people with accessibility challenges to explore cultural heritage
  • A digital humanitarian aid platform

Texas A&M University at Qatar (TAMUQ) equipped its student leaders with skills that can enhance the role they play in campus life, while Georgetown University in Qatar welcomed Pulitzer Prize-winning author Nathan Thrall – who claimed the accolade for a book examining the plight of Palestinians – and HEC Paris, Doha launched its Business Research Lab, the aim of which is to help ensure the regional relevancy of its executive education programs.

70
student leaders representing 22 student organizations participated in TAMUQ's Student Leadership Summit

January also saw QF-founded Qatar Career Development Center collaborate with the Ministry of Education and Higher Education to host key discussions on establishing a national career development ecosystem for Qatar.

And QF's global education thinktank WISE, together with HBKU's College of Public Policy, launched 'Action Plan for an Inclusive Education Policy for the State of Qatar' – a research report developed by WISE and the University of Cambridge, highlighting national successes in supporting people with disabilities to access education, and the challenges that must still be addressed.

Social Progress

Taking the Plunge

And QF's commitment to accessibility and inclusivity saw it really make a splash in January – as its Ability Friendly program teamed up with the Qatar Paralympic Committee to host its first swimming competition for people with disabilities.

Launched in 2018, the popular and growing program within QF's Pre-University Education provides tailored sports and developmental activities for children and adults with disabilities – with football programs and specialized camps alongside its regular swimming sessions.

It operates six days a week in Education City, and has branched out to also offer classes twice-weekly in Al Khor and Al Wakra, as well as enhancing educational programs for coaches and amassing a dedicated squad of vital volunteers.

"Participating in competitions not only enhances athletes' technical abilities and knowledge, but also contributes to their social development and personal growth," said Rodesa Comendador, sports coach with the Ability Friendly Program.

"The program's goal is to advance the accessibility movement across all sports. As we continue to grow, we are committed to creating more opportunities for individuals with disabilities to thrive in sports, both competitively and recreationally."
